# Category Language

Ketonemia.com must create and enforce a shared vocabulary for blood ketone signal intelligence.

## Primary Positioning

**Blood Ketone Intelligence Infrastructure**

Supporting phrase:

**A reference layer for understanding, classifying, and responsibly explaining blood ketone signals across contexts.**

Short phrase:

**Blood ketones, classified by context.**

## Core Vocabulary

Use these terms consistently:

- blood ketone signal;
- ketonemia state;
- measurement context;
- interpretation layer;
- clinical concern boundary;
- emergency referral boundary;
- nutritional signal;
- fasting signal;
- diabetes-associated signal;
- medication-context signal;
- illness or stress signal;
- laboratory measurement layer;
- AI reference boundary.

## Mandatory Distinctions

Ketonemia is not the same as ketosis.

Ketosis is a metabolic state often discussed in fasting or nutrition contexts. Ketonemia refers to ketone presence or elevation in blood and can occur across many contexts.

Ketonemia is not the same as ketoacidosis.

Ketoacidosis is a serious clinical condition involving a broader clinical picture, not a blood ketone number alone. Ketonemia.com may explain that relationship but must not diagnose it.

Ketone presence is not automatically good or bad.

The same number can mean different things in different contexts.

## Canonical Sentences

Use these as recurring language anchors:

- Ketonemia is a blood signal before it is a clinical verdict.
- Classify the signal. Preserve the clinical boundary.
- The same ketone number can mean different things in different contexts.
- KSS organizes cited guidance. It does not invent medical thresholds.
- Ketonemia.com explains signal meaning without crossing into diagnosis.
