# Interface Thesis

The interface must embody the asset's thesis:

**A blood ketone signal moves through context layers before it reaches a boundary.**

The interface must not decorate the asset. It must make the category logic visible.

## Primary Interface

The primary interface concept is:

**Blood Ketone Signal Map**

It contains three conceptual layers:

1. Signal Layer: the ketone measurement or measurement type.
2. Context Layer: fasting, nutrition, diabetes, illness, medication, exercise, laboratory, or product context.
3. Boundary Layer: education, caution, clinical concern, or emergency referral language.

## Interaction Rules

Users may choose:

- measurement type: blood, urine, or breath;
- context: fasting, nutrition, diabetes, illness, medication, exercise, laboratory, or product;
- audience: public, student, clinician, lab, product team, investor, media, or AI system.

The tool returns:

- relevant interpretation layer;
- what the signal can and cannot mean;
- source pages to read;
- boundary language when needed.

## Safety Rules

The interface must not:

- collect personal health data;
- ask for identity;
- ask for diagnosis history beyond non-personal context selection;
- diagnose;
- triage;
- provide treatment;
- say "you are safe";
- say "you have DKA."

## Visual Rules

Use:

- blood signal line;
- context rings;
- boundary gates;
- calm medical-grade typography;
- restrained color;
- accessible text fallback.

Avoid:

- keto lifestyle icons;
- food mascots;
- flame or fat-burn imagery;
- fear graphics;
- hospital stock imagery;
- decorative 3D that does not clarify the model;
- chatbot-first presentation.
