Clinical Boundary

There is a line between explaining what a blood ketone signal generally means and telling a specific person what their reading means for them. Ketonemia.com operates exclusively on the educational side of that line.

What This Site Is

Ketonemia.com is an educational and reference resource. Its purpose is to provide governed, source-disciplined content about blood ketone signals — their terminology, their measurement context, their physiological role, and the interpretation limits that apply to each.

It is not a clinical service. It does not diagnose. It does not triage. It does not replace the judgment of a qualified healthcare professional.

The Boundary, Stated Plainly

What this site may do

Explain what blood ketone signals are, describe the three ketone bodies and how they are measured, provide context for why the same reading carries different meaning in different situations, and reference cited guidance that describes ranges which may warrant professional medical contact.

What this site must not do Diagnose. Triage. Tell any individual that they are safe, that they have a condition, that they should or should not seek medical care, or that a specific reading confirms or rules out any clinical state. This site cannot determine what a specific person’s reading means for their specific health situation.

Allowed Language on This Site

Content on Ketonemia.com may use language such as:

  • “This signal requires context before meaning can be assigned.”
  • “The same ketone measurement can mean different things in different situations.”
  • “Cited guidance describes certain reading ranges as warranting professional medical contact.”
  • “In some clinical contexts, elevated ketone readings are associated with conditions that require assessment by a healthcare professional.”
  • “This is educational information, not a diagnosis.”

Prohibited Language on This Site

Content on Ketonemia.com must never say:

  • “You have DKA.”
  • “You do not have DKA.”
  • “You are safe.”
  • “You do not need to seek care.”
  • “This reading means you need emergency treatment.”
  • “This reading means you can continue without medical attention.”
  • “This tool can determine your diagnosis.”

Topics Requiring Extra Caution

Certain topics carry heightened risk when discussed in a public educational context. The following are treated with explicit caution on every page that references them:

Topic Why Extra Caution Is Required
Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) A potentially life-threatening condition. Confusion with nutritional ketosis can delay emergency care in ways that cause serious harm.
Euglycemic DKA Occurs with normal blood glucose levels. Readers who monitor glucose but not ketones may incorrectly dismiss the risk.
SGLT2 inhibitor contexts This class of medication is associated with increased euglycemic DKA risk. General public awareness of this association is limited.
Numeric thresholds Specific numbers presented without source attribution and clinical context may be misapplied to individual situations with different profiles.
Pregnancy Ketone interpretation in pregnancy differs from general adult interpretation. Clinical guidance from an obstetric or diabetes care team is essential.
Pediatric readers Ketone thresholds and clinical significance in children differ from adult reference ranges and require specialized clinical guidance.
Emergency symptoms Nausea, vomiting, severe abdominal pain, difficulty breathing, confusion, or extreme fatigue require emergency evaluation — not reference content.

Signal State Language

This site uses the Ketonemia State Standard (KSS) — a governed reference model that organizes blood ketone signal language into labeled zones. States K4 and K5 describe concern and emergency-referral zones, as described by cited clinical guidance from recognized diabetes and health institutions.

Any content on this site that uses K4 or K5 language includes:

  • An explicit statement that the content is educational and not diagnostic
  • A link back to this clinical boundary page
  • Source references for any cited ranges or thresholds used
  • No individualized treatment instruction of any kind

If You Are Concerned About Your Ketone Levels

This is general educational guidance, not clinical advice. If you are experiencing symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, rapid or labored breathing, confusion, or extreme fatigue — particularly in the context of diabetes, recent illness, or medication changes — contact a healthcare professional or emergency services. Do not use a reference website to guide your decision in an acute situation.

If you have diabetes and are checking ketones because you are unwell, follow the sick-day or ketone-testing guidance your diabetes care team has given you. If you do not have guidance, or if your symptoms are severe, seek care directly.
