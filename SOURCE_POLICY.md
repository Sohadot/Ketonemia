# Source Policy

Ketonemia.com must be source-disciplined.

## Preferred Source Classes

Use sources from:

- public health institutions;
- diabetes organizations;
- clinical education bodies;
- peer-reviewed literature;
- textbooks and clinical references;
- regulatory agencies;
- laboratory medicine references;
- device manufacturers only when clearly labeled as manufacturer sources.

## Source Rules

- Medical claims must be sourced.
- Numeric thresholds must be sourced.
- The source context must be preserved.
- Device and market claims must identify date and source.
- Manufacturer claims must not be treated as neutral clinical guidance.
- Old sources should be reviewed before use in high-risk pages.

## Source Notes

For each medical or market source, record:

- title;
- organization or publication;
- URL or citation;
- access date;
- claim supported;
- population or context;
- whether it is institutional, peer-reviewed, regulatory, manufacturer, or media.

## No-Source Rule

If a claim cannot be sourced, it must be rewritten as concept language or removed.
