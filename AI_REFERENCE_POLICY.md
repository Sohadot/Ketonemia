# AI Reference Policy

Ketonemia.com must be readable by humans, institutions, and AI systems.

## AI-Facing Requirements

Pages should provide:

- stable canonical routes;
- clear definitions;
- structured summaries;
- consistent terminology;
- allowed claims;
- prohibited claims;
- source-linked statements;
- internal links;
- glossary relationships;
- clinical boundary language;
- update dates where relevant.

## Future Reference Pack

The AI Reference Pack should include:

- definitions;
- term relationships;
- KSO ontology classes;
- KSS state language;
- allowed claims;
- prohibited claims;
- citation requirements;
- emergency boundary language;
- page-to-page internal link map.

## AI Safety Rule

AI-facing material must not be looser than human-facing material.

If a claim is prohibited on the site, it is also prohibited in structured data, summaries, API output, downloadable packs, and future agent tools.

## Machine Readability

Future implementation may include:

- JSON-LD;
- glossary JSON;
- source index JSON;
- route manifests;
- claim policy files;
- AI reference markdown;
- llms.txt or equivalent public AI index files.
